Hi! I'm Erica! I have 5 years of professional experience in coaching chess, leading some talented juniors to first place finishes at the New York State Scholastic Chess Championships.

I'm currently a B.Sc. Computer Science candidate @ the University of Rochester who's interested in equities research, especially concerning Asian markets.
This is site was primarily to introduce prospective chess students to my qualifications, though my blog will be linked as well.
I plan to graduate from the UofR in 2025 with an Computer Science degree. To broaden my understanding of world markets. I've also been an analyst in the University at Albany's investment group. We are currently funded by the Center for Institutional Investment Management. Our objective is to actively manage the university portfolio and outperform the S&P1500 index by adhering to strict technical analysis to create a long-term investment strategy.
This experience has been really eye-opening in terms of how professional analysts work. It sunk in that people are stratified into those who understand the financial systems implemented by governments and corporations, and those who don't. I personally believe that financial literacy of any degree is useful in all aspects of practical life, but I want to take this further and make this my profession as to become fluent in the industry's methods of financial analysis. The more value I bring to the table, the better.
As of now, my professional experience is limited to a few internships, the most fulfilling of which may have been in risk management at a public pension fund. The experience led to me to become interested in exploring more quantitative investment methods. However, the university investment group provides ample pracical experiences in financial modeling with Excel. In the fall semester of 2021, I analyzed ASML holdings, a Dutch corporation in the semiconductor industry specializing in photolithography equipment, as well as Builder's First Source, an American manufacturer and supplier of building materials. By creating an operating model and analyzing financial records, I recommended these stocks to be added to the university portfolio.
I have linked our current holdings. Our equity report is updated monthly. This will be updated to the best of my ability.
Besides the current work I'm doing, I'm interested in the Asian equities market, and hope to get the chance to work internationally. Gaining and providing a more comprehensive perspective of this market is key to protecting an individuals' welfare in a global economy. With this knowledge, I can more effectively evaluate and apply non-financial factors, such as geopolitics and culture, to my financial analysis.
All in all, I hope that this course of study brings me fulfillment, and brings value to those around me.
I'll be linking my ACG blog here for all interested parties. The semester has been so busy, and I've made no progress with any A, C or G since September! My most recent exploit was in Youth with You 2. I'll try and provide some sort of consistent and meaningful commentary on Asian Pop Culture soon. I recently had the opportunity to see AKB48 Team SH in Guangzhou as well. In the meantime, hang tight.
If you are here for chess, you've come to the right place. I've been an avid chess fan almost 7 years, and was truly hooked by the competitive atmosphere of rated chess tournaments. If you put in the work and muster up the passion you'll be beating the big dogs in no time!
Huge shoutout to the organization: Make the Right Move. which has been providing free chess tournaments in upstate New York since 2004!!!!
Even if you don't choose me as your coach, if you live in this area I seriously recommend joining these tournaments either in-person or online. They are a great experience, and there is a strong pool of players.
My coaching methods mostly comprise of puzzle training and book homework. Each curriculum is tailored to the specific students' level and needs. I usually reserve half an hour for scrimmages after lessons.
